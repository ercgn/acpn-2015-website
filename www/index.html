Event Information

All Campus Praise Night is one night where Christians from all over CMU
come together to praise the one true living God of the Bible.
This year, the event will be held on Aug 30, 8 PM @ The Fence.

Who is like You, O Lord, among the gods? Who is like You, glorious in holiness, fearful in praises, doing wonders?
(Exodus 15:11)

Our theme this year is HOLY. We want to emphasize this aspect of God's nature, pointing Christians back to praising our holy God. We want Christians to be convicted of their sin. We want Christians to live holy lives, because God is holy.

“The clearest sensation that a human being has when he experiences the holy is an overpowering and overwhelming sense of creatureliness. That is, when we are in the presence of God, we are humbled and become most aware of ourselves as creatures. This is the opposite of Satan's original temptation, ‘You shall be as gods.’”
― R.C. Sproul, The Holiness of God

God is holy

And one cried to another and said: “Holy, holy, holy is the Lord of hosts; The whole earth is full of His glory!”
(Isaiah 6:3)

We are called to be holy

But as He who called you is holy, you also be holy in all your conduct, because it is written, “Be holy, for I am holy.”
(1 Peter 1:15-16)

Holiness means …

What does God's holiness mean to you? How will you fulfill your calling to be holy? How will your spur others to be holy? These are questions that we want Christians to think about throughout the night.

Volunteering

Praise

Have a heart for worship and a talent for singing or an instrument? We need musicians and singers. There will be at least four practices, spread across the oWo week and the week before Praise Night.

Operations

Operations includes logistics and tech support. These are the unheard and unseen servants in almost every event. Logistics involves moving instruments and equipment to and from the venue. Tech support involves making slides, operating the laptop, and manning the mixer. You must commit to arriving early and leaving later to help with set-up and tear-down.

Hospitality

Hospitality includes publicity and outreach. We need people to get the word out about Praise Night. We also need welcomers to welcome attendees and hand out name tags, as well as ushers to collect committment cards.

Prayer

We believe in the importance of prayer. We need ‘united, persistent, and kingdom centered’ prayers to be lifted up to God in preparation for this event. We need people from all fellowrships at CMU who can commit to praying individually but unitedly that God will use All Campus Praise Night for His glory.
